Honda Owners Warranty



Honda Warranty Coverage Every new Honda comes with a factory warranty to ensure your vehicle is covered in the case of a manufacturer or workmanship error. Factory warranties are broken down into two components: the basic coverage warranty, often referred to as the bumper-to-bumper warranty, and the powertrain warranty. The American Honda Motor Co. offers these two warranties along with several more. The breakdown of Honda’s warranty offering for its new vehicles is as follows.

New Vehicle Limited Warranty If you’re purchasing a new Honda, you automatically receive a New Vehicle Limited Warranty, which covers many components, including the air conditioning unit, battery, audio system, and other electrical components. The coverage is good for three years or 36,000 miles, whichever comes first.

Additional limited coverage includes:
Key fob and remote batteries—6 months Wiper blade inserts—6 months Wheel balancing and wheel alignment—1 year or 12,000 miles Air conditioner refrigerant—2 years or 24,000 miles Limited Powertrain Warranty The powertrain is your car’s powerhouse. The car’s powertrain system includes the following parts:
Engine Transmission and transaxle Drivetrain Restraint system Powertrain warranties cover everything involving the repair and replacement of these parts and other parts included in the powertrain. Honda’s warranty for your powertrain is good for either five years or 60,000 miles, whichever comes first.
Honda’s Limited Powertrain Warranty protects the following:
Engine Engine-related repairs include work on the cylinder block and head and all internal parts; timing gears and gaskets; timing chain/belt and cover; flywheel; valve covers; oil pan and oil pump; intake and exhaust manifolds; engine mounts; engine/powertrain control module; water pump; fuel pump; seals, and gaskets. Transmission and Transaxle Honda’s transmission and transaxle repairs include case and all internal parts; torque converter; transfer case and all internal parts; transmission/powertrain control module; seals, and gaskets. Front-Wheel Drive System Honda’s Limited Powertrain Warranty services the following components of the front-wheel drive system: final drive housing and all internal parts; driveshafts; constant velocity joints; front hubs and bearings; seals, and gaskets. Rear-Wheel Drive System Honda’s Rear-Wheel-Drive System coverage includes the differential housing and all internal parts; propeller shafts; universal joints; driveshafts; constant velocity joints; rear hubs and bearings; seals, and gaskets. Accessory Limited Warranty The Honda warranty offers three years, or 36,000 miles of protection for Honda Genuine Accessories installed at the time of the vehicle’s purchase. According to the warranty booklet, in addition to the accessories included in the purchase, Honda also grants warranties to accessories purchased through, but not installed by, Honda dealers. All accessories included at the time of purchase are covered by either the time remaining in the New Vehicle Limited Warranty or one year, whichever is longer. Accessories purchased through a Honda dealer are also protected for one year.

Replacement Parts Limited Warranty Honda provides coverage for its Honda Genuine Parts for one year from the date of purchase of the duration of the New Vehicle Warranty (three years or 36,000 miles), whichever is longer. The Replacement/Remanufactured Parts Limited Warranty only covers repair claims that include documented proof of purchase. It only covers parts considered to be normal maintenance items, like spark plugs, filters, and brake pads, if they are defective.

Rust Perforation Limited Warranty Honda’s Rust Perforation Limited Warranty, or corrosion warranty, provides protection against rust and deterioration for original body panels. This Honda warranty is good for five years and unlimited mileage. It does not protect against the rusting of body panels that were repaired, replaced, or refinished after the purchase of your vehicle.

Roadside Assistance Honda offers 24-hour emergency roadside assistance for the first three years or 36,000 miles of your vehicle. Honda’s 24-hour emergency roadside assistance covers the following: Jump start Fuel delivery Lockouts Flat tire Mechanical tow Accident tow Winching Tire Warranty Coverage for your tires is separate from your Honda warranty—they’re covered by the tire manufacturer. Look online for your specific tire warranty from Yokohoma, Bridgestone/Firestone, Hankook, Maxxis, Continental, JK, Michelin, Goodyear/Dunlop, or Kenda.

Additional Coverage Honda cars less than three years old with fewer than 36,000 miles are eligible for additional coverage with Honda Care® Vehicle Service Contract (VSC) Plans. If your Honda vehicle is less than one year old and has fewer than 12,000 miles, it is eligible for Honda Care Maintenance™. These aftermarket warranties provide prepaid services, rental car reimbursements, and more. What Your Honda Limited Warranty Doesn’t Cover The New Vehicle Limited Warranty does not cover normal wear or deterioration of any part, cleaning and polishing, or adding any fluids, unless they are needed as part of a warranty repair. Unless broken, chipped, or scratched window glass is due to a defect in material or workmanship, it will not be covered. Cosmetic flaws, such as paint, that aren’t due to a defect in material or workmanship will also not be covered under the New Vehicle Limited Warranty. In addition, the Honda warranty won’t cover damage or failure of parts or accessories due to:

Improper maintenance or installation Abuse or misuse Accidental damage Low fluid levels Racing Modifications Honda also won’t honor the warranty if the odometer has been altered or if the vehicle has been declared a total loss. Honda Warranties that Go the Extra Mile: Extended Warranties While Honda’s warranty program sounds generous on the surface, it actually lags behind that of other manufacturers.

For example, these car brands provide longer warranties than Honda: Make Limited Basic Warranty Acura 4 years, 50,000 miles BMW 4 years, 50,000 miles Volkswagen 4 years, 60,000 miles Kia 5 years, 60,000 miles Mitsubishi 5 years, 60,000 miles The Kia and Mitsubishi limited powertrain warranties last up to 10 years or 100,000 miles, almost double what the Honda powertrain warranty offers. If you’re buying a Honda vehicle, maximize your investment and consider an extended auto warranty from a third-party company like CARCHEX. Why Consider an Extended Warranty? An extended warranty is a smart decision to give your vehicle—and wallet—the coverage it needs. It can protect you from the inevitable costly repairs your Honda will need as it gets into higher mileage territory, especially after its manufacturer warranty expires. When it comes to purchasing an extended warranty, you have two options: the dealership or a third-party warranty company. If you purchase a Honda extended warranty from the dealership, you are limited to their levels of protection, which give you fewer options in the long run. You are also only able to take your vehicle to a Honda dealer for repairs.
